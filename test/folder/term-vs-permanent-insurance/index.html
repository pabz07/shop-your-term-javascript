Permanent Life Insurance: Term Life with Added Benefits You Keep for Life

Discover another type of life insurance that combines term life with a unique blend of security features and savings growth potential.

When you purchase permanent life insurance, you're essentially committing to lifelong coverage that guarantees protection for as long as you live, provided that premiums are consistently paid. This long-term commitment means your coverage never expires, offering you invaluable peace of mind knowing that your loved ones will always be financially protected, regardless of when you pass away.

This investment vehicle allows you to accumulate cash value over time, which can grow at a rate comparable to returns seen in the stock market—but without exposing you to the direct risks typically associated with stock market fluctuations.

Having a policy that not only safeguards your beneficiaries but also contributes to your wealth can be a game-changer in your overall financial strategy.

Term vs. Permanent Life Insurance

Term Life Insurance

Term life insurance is a powerful  protection tool that provides coverage for a specified period, paying a death benefit to beneficiaries who can use the money to replace an income, pay off a mortgage, cover debts and funeral expenses, and avoid financial hardship.

Advantages

  • Simplicity
    Term Life Insurance only offers a death benefit, which makes the process of structuring and purchasing it straight forward.

  • AffordabilityThe cost of term is the affordable type life insurance due to its single feature of a death benefit.

  • Simplified UnderwritingAllows for quicker approvals and easier access to coverage by requiring minimal health information and bypassing the need for a medical exam.

  • Living BenefitsA coverage add-on that allows you or your beneficiaries to access benefits while they’re still alive, like money for illness expenses.

Permanent Life Insurance

Permanent life is essentially term insurance that combines lifelong coverage with a savings component that accumulates cash value over time, providing both a death benefit and a vehicle to build financial security and independence.

The best way to determine which type is most suitable for your individual needs is to consult with a financial professional, who can provide personalized suggestions based on your financial situation, long-term goals, and specific circumstances, ensuring that you make an informed choice that aligns with your priorities.
